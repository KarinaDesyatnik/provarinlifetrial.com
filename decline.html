Stages of Sexual Function

Every man needs to possess the three sexual health markers in order to participate in sex: libido, erections, and sexual stamina. These markers can become weaker with age, lifestyle factors, and progression through the three stages of sexual function.

Stage 1

Sexual Prime

(age 18-30)

  • Hard, fast and sometimes sporadic erections
  • Strong libido and sex-drive
  • Long-lasting erections and sexual energy

Stage 2

Sexual Decline

(age 30-70)

  • Softer, more difficult to achieve erections
  • Reduced libido, desire for sex
  • Reduced stamina and ability to maintain erections

Stage 3

Sexual Dysfunction

(age 50+)

  • Failure to achieve erections
  • Very low or almost non-existent libido
  • Failure to maintain erections and very low sexual energy.

When any or all of the three sexual health markers start functioning at less than healthy, optimal levels, it could be a sign of Sexual Decline.

What Causes Sexual Decline Symptoms?

When bio-drivers aren’t functioning at their peaks, the physical symptoms of sexual decline occur. Reduction of these bio-drivers, which leads to the breakdown of the three sexual health markers, can be caused by a variety of age and lifestyle factors, including stress, smoking, diet, quality of sleep, medical conditions, and certain medications.

The Downward Spiral Of Sexual Decline.
Causes
Age, smoking, lifestyle factors, and medical conditions.
Effects
Reduction of bio-drivers testosterone, nitric oxide, blood flow, and metabolic efficiency.
Consequences
Breakdown of sexual health markers libido, arousal quality, and sexual stamina.
Outcomes
Decline in sexual function, strength, performance, and enjoyment.

Testosterone levels drop A reduced libido, desire for sex, and overall sexual performance can be caused by low levels of testosterone, which can go down 2-4% every year after age 35 based on lifestyle habits and overall health.

BLOOD FLOW DECREASES By age 40, men produce up to 55% less nitric oxide than they did in their prime, which leads to softer erections that can be more difficult to achieve and maintain. This may be due to a number of factors, including certain lifestyle risks, as well as other medical conditions. (diabetes, high blood pressure, and cholesterol or lipid problems).

METABOLISM SLOWS A sluggish and inefficient metabolism will reduce sexual stamina and the ability to maintain erections. A man’s metabolism naturally declines slowly with age based on certain lifestyle factors. Once he hits age 50, the rate of decline rapidly increases, causing a loss of energy, stamina, and sexual endurance.
